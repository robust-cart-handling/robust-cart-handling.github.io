Task-Space RL Trajectory Correction for Robust Cart Handling with Bimanual Mobile Manipulation

Anonymous authors
Supplement arXiv Video

Abstract

Cart manipulation is a challenging mobile manipulation task affected by uncertain friction, uneven payloads, and complex motion modes. Moreover, the end-effector-handle interactions exhibit diverse patterns, including spacing, sticking, and sliding, all of which are difficult to model. To address these challenges, we propose a task-space reinforcement learning (RL) framework that generates real-time corrections to nominal end-effector (EE) trajectories, thereby optimizing contact behavior and improving tracking performance without relying on precise dynamic models. Specifically, the framework integrates (i) a task-space floating EE controller for RL training and whole-body deployment, (ii) a piecewise random trajectory generator that unifies pushing, pulling, and steering within a single policy, and (iii) a tailored curriculum learning and domain randomization scheme to enhance cross-scenario generalization. Extensive simulations and real-world experiments demonstrate that the proposed method reduces cart trajectory tracking errors by nearly 50% compared with evaluated baselines and achieves performance comparable to skilled human operators.

Method

The proposed hierarchical framework couples a high-level RL reference correction strategy with low-level model-based controllers for bimanual cart manipulation. The RL policy (25Hz) takes the nominal cart trajectory and observations as inputs and outputs the EE perturbations as corrections. Then, the desired EE trajectories are updated by the perturbations. The two dashed boxes present the low-level control frameworks tracking the desired EE trajectories in task space. The floating EE controller (200Hz) supports parallel RL training in task space with real-time error feedback, while the whole-body tracker (50Hz) coordinates the upper-body and chassis motions and enables physical deployment. Their decoupled design allows the RL policy to focus on high-level reference correction, while the low-level controllers enforce kinematic and dynamic constraints and compensate for disturbances.

Experiment Settings

Predefined Trajectories

We evaluate all methods on five predefined nominal trajectories. Specifically, TrajA–D are generated from trapezoidal profiles of the cart local velocities with Rmin = 2 m, while TrajE is a Lissajous-like trajectory with Rmin = 0.5 m, involving frequent heading changes.
