Task-Space RL-Based Trajectory Correction for Robust Cart Handling with Bimanual Mobile Manipulators

Anonymous authors
Supplement arXiv Video

Abstract

Cart handling is a challenging mobile manipulation task affected by uncertain friction, uneven payloads, and complex motion modes. Moreover, the end-effector-handle interactions feature diverse patterns, including spacing, sticking, and sliding, which are difficult to model. To address these challenges, we propose a task-space reinforcement learning (RL) framework that generates real-time corrections to nominal end-effector (EE) trajectories, thereby optimizing contact behavior and improving tracking performance without relying on precise dynamic models. Specifically, the framework integrates (i) a task-space floating EE controller for RL training and whole-body deployment, (ii) a piecewise random trajectory generator that unifies pushing, pulling, and steering within a single policy, and (iii) a tailored curriculum learning and domain randomization scheme to enhance cross-scenario generalization. Extensive real-world experiments demonstrate that the proposed method reduces cart trajectory tracking errors by nearly 30% compared with evaluated baselines and achieves performance comparable to skilled human operators.

Method

The proposed hierarchical framework couples a high-level RL trajectory correction strategy with low-level model-based controllers for bimanual cart manipulation. The RL policy (25Hz) takes the nominal cart trajectory and observations as inputs and outputs the EE perturbations as corrections. Then, the desired EE trajectories are updated by the perturbations and are tracked by two low-level controllers. The floating EE controller (200Hz) supports parallel RL training in task space with real-time feedback, while the whole-body tracker (50Hz) coordinates the upper-body and chassis motions for physical deployment. The system forms a cascaded two-loop structure: the outer RL loop generates corrections based on tracking feedback, while the inner loop executes the motion. This design allows the RL policy to focus on high-level trajectory correction, while the low-level controllers enforce kinodynamic constraints and compensate for disturbances.

Experiments

Predefined Trajectories

We evaluate all methods on five predefined nominal trajectories. Specifically, TrajA--D are generated from trapezoidal velocity profiles at three graded velocity levels in the cart local frame with Rmin = 2 m, while TrajE is a Lissajous-like trajectory with Rmin = 0.5 m, involving frequent heading changes.
